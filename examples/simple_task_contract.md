# Simple Task Contract: Market Research Memo

## Goal

Generate and review a short market research memo about adoption trends for developer productivity tools among small software teams.

## Context

- Audience: a product lead deciding whether to fund further research.
- Length: 600 to 900 words.
- Time horizon: evidence published within the last 24 months.
- This is an exploratory memo, not investment, legal, or procurement advice.

## Constraints

- Use only public sources.
- Distinguish sourced facts from inference.
- Do not invent market size, adoption, revenue, or user figures.
- Include links or citations for factual claims.
- State uncertainty and important missing evidence.
- Do not include personal data or private company material.

## Required Outputs

1. Executive summary.
2. Three supported adoption observations.
3. Two counter-signals or reasons for caution.
4. Implications for a small product team.
5. Source list.
6. Reviewer notes listing unsupported or weak claims.

## Quality Gate

- Every quantitative claim has a source.
- Sources fall within the declared time horizon or are labeled as background.
- Facts and inference are visibly separated.
- At least one credible counterargument is included.
- No invented traction or market-size claims appear.
- An independent review pass identifies weak evidence before acceptance.

## Failure Memory Update

If the memo fails review, record:

- The unsupported or misleading claim.
- Why it appeared plausible.
- Which source or reasoning step was missing.
- A preventive rule for the next run.
- Whether the contract or quality gate needs revision.

Example preventive rule:

> Do not convert survey percentages into total-market estimates unless the sampling method and population support that inference.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The memo is between 600 and 900 words.
- All six required outputs are present.
- Every quality-gate item passes or has an explicit human-approved waiver.
- The final memo can be reviewed without access to the originating chat.
