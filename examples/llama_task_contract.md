# Llama Task Contract: Creator Content Planning Memo

## Goal

Create and review a concise content planning memo for an independent creator preparing a four-week educational series about practical software craftsmanship.

## Context

- Audience: early-career developers.
- Channels: a weekly article and a short companion post.
- Tone: practical, calm, and evidence-aware.
- The memo should guide planning; it should not generate the final published content.
- Model runtime: a Llama-powered execution and review workflow.

## Model Role

### Execution Role

Draft the planning memo using the contract and required outputs below. Mark assumptions and identify missing information.

### Review Role

Independently evaluate the draft against the Quality Gate. Do not rewrite the memo until the review findings are recorded.

## Constraints

- Use only public, non-sensitive context.
- Do not invent audience size, engagement, revenue, or performance claims.
- Keep the plan feasible for one creator working part-time.
- Separate factual background from creative recommendations.
- Avoid naming private collaborators, clients, or unpublished projects.
- Escalate unresolved audience or publishing constraints to human review.

## Required Outputs

1. Series objective.
2. Audience definition.
3. Four weekly themes.
4. Article and companion-post format for each week.
5. Source or evidence needs.
6. Production checklist.
7. Risks and open questions.
8. Independent review findings.

## Quality Gate

- The objective and audience are explicit.
- All four weeks support the same series goal.
- Each week has a distinct learning outcome.
- The workload is plausible for the stated capacity.
- Recommendations are not presented as measured facts.
- No private information appears.
- The review identifies at least one assumption and one execution risk.
- Human approval is recorded before the plan becomes a publishing commitment.

## Failure Memory Update

If the memo fails the gate, record:

- Failed criterion.
- Observable defect.
- Why the draft appeared reasonable.
- Missing context or constraint.
- Preventive rule for the next run.
- Whether the contract, skill, or review rubric should change.

Example preventive rule:

> A four-week creator plan must include a workload estimate before it can pass feasibility review.

## Acceptance Criteria

- All eight required outputs are present.
- Every Quality Gate item passes or has an explicit human-approved waiver.
- Execution and review outputs are distinguishable.
- The memo can be understood without access to the originating conversation.
- No claim implies endorsement by Meta or affiliation with the Llama ecosystem.
